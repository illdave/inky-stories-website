I was born in America between Y2K and 9/11, the youngest of seven siblings (five girls and two boys). We didn't have a lot of money but somehow got by. As my friends got priced out, I became the new girl in my own goddamn neighborhood! The new kids wanted nothing to do with my brown-skin family. I hated my a deeply religious, stay-at-home mother. She paid the bills, managed dad's business, and took care of us kids, but her bipolar severe beatings/weepy apology cycle were dramatic even by standards. At eight years old, I asked mom if she was jealous of me because I was younger and prettier. She nearly killed me. The only things I admired about that woman were her beauty and ability to hide her violent streak in public. To this day no one believes my accounts of that house of horrors.

Luckily I was dad's favorite. He was the neighborhood fix-it man. I learned that anything can be built or repaired with ingenuity and the right parts, even if sometimes those parts "fell off a truck." Gentrification replaced dad's "enthusiastic" DIY spirit with professionals who actually knew what they were doing.
