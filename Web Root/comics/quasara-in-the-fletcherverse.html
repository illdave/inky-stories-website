About This Page

Hart-Nell is based William Hartnell's personality on Doctor Who, retaining all his negative traits but none of the virtues. Like every Hanks villain, he feels mass murder is a justifiable, if not desirable, instrument of revenge.

He might've built that wrist device on his own, but I personally think he forcibly stole it from Space Ghost.

The fire breathing, bat winged demon shrimp is a Hanks-inspired bug-eyed monster (BEM).

About This Page

Trying to tell a story with Hanks' actual panel compositions nearly drove me insane. Redefining the mission from strict to interpretive allowed the use of modern approaches to visual storytelling and page layout. I also gave up "dumbing down" my drafting, but tried my best to make it look like Hanks inked it.

This village scene is the first example of expressing today's dramatic naturalism in a Fletcher Hanks style. I'm still proud of it.

Story Notes

This project began after revisiting the 1939-1941 work of Fletcher Hanks, whose Fantomah and Stardust the Super Wizard stories have been described as "divine justice of the weirdo gods." His work was substandard even for the era, yet I'm fascinated by the ferocious eccentricity of his visual language.

Reading the excellent Fantagraphics collections compelled me to create a Fletcher Hanks story with Null Device characters and modern perspective. Satire was one of my goals; mimicking his visual and narrative decisions to expose everything wrong with Hanks and his work. I also needed this to read as a pure Fletcher Hanks story, hinting at his personal shortcomings while retaining his self righteous violence and visual absurdity.

The process was a page-by-page challenge, which I now think was mostly successful. Hope you feel the same.
