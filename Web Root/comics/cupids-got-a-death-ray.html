About This Page

The front cover is based on Marvel's shell of the mid-1970s, lifting the logo block graphics from Inky Stories #5. The 3D "Inky Stories Presents," based on the old Marvel Premier logo, was rendered by hand in Adobe Illustrator.

The cover illustration was originally going to be drawn by Line Olsson from my thumbnail sketch. Deadlines and logistics prevented that from happening. I ending up drawing and coloring the final art, trying to retain the best of Line's ideas (crosshatching, Agent K's hair, and goth Cupid). Drawing the final art took about 5 hours, and another 10 to color (flatting, coloring, and the flat CMYK dot color emulation).

The cover uses the Bronze Age 128-color palette, rather than the Golden/Silver Age 64-color palette of my earlier dot color comics.

About This Page

There wasn't time to collaborate with Line on this, so I did my best to incorporate here style, right down to the signature. I mistakenly based Agent K's face on Line's round head style; she drew her with an angular face in the story. Consecutive late-night drawing sessions often lead to sloppy decisions. The art, writing, and lettering were done between midnight and 9:00am.

Story Notes

Exploring the comics art form itself, Cupid tells the same story from two characters point of view. It's designed to read horizontally and vertically. Each narrative is told in a different art style. Imagine Alex Toth and Edward Gorey telling the same story. Their storylines merge at the end, like superheroes joining forces after their in-fighting wrecks a city or two.

Not bad for eight pages.

Line Olsson is an illustrator, chalkboard artist and cartoonist. Her of exaggerated, intricate approach to sequential storytelling makes Line a perfect fit for Agent K, who Line describes as "a badass action hero." We "jammed" on this project whenever possible, drawing and inking over each other's work.

Dave M!, blogging from 1369 Coffee House, Inman Square.
